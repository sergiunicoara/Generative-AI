AIRCRAFT TYPE PROFILE
Airbus A320neo (New Engine Option) Family

Manufacturer: Airbus SAS
Headquarters: 2 Rond-Point Emile Dewoitine, 31707 Blagnac, France
ICAO Manufacturer Code: AIB
Type Certificate: EASA.A.064

---

OVERVIEW

The Airbus A320neo (New Engine Option) family is a series of narrow-body commercial
aircraft manufactured by Airbus. The "neo" designation refers to the new, more
fuel-efficient engine options compared to the original A320ceo (Current Engine Option)
family. The A320neo family entered commercial service in January 2016.

---

AIRCRAFT VARIANTS

A319neo
Capacity: 120-160 passengers (typical 2-class)
Range: 3,750 nm
Engine options: CFM International LEAP-1A, Pratt & Whitney PW1100G

A320-200N (A320neo)
Capacity: 150-194 passengers (typical 2-class)
Range: 3,400 nm
Engine options: CFM International LEAP-1A, Pratt & Whitney PW1100G
Key structural changes: Sharklet wingtip devices, reinforced landing gear

A321neo
Capacity: 180-220 passengers
Range: 4,000 nm (A321XLR variant: 5,400 nm)
Engine options: CFM International LEAP-1A, Pratt & Whitney PW1100G

CERTIFICATION AND REGULATORY STATUS

EASA Type Certificate: EASA.A.064 (A320 family, includes neo variants)
FAA Type Certificate: A28NM (validated by FAA for US operations)
TCCA: Approved
CAAC: Approved

The A320neo family is subject to continuous airworthiness oversight by EASA,
with applicable ADs issued under EASA AD authority. Key ADs include:

EASA AD 2024-0072 (effective March 29, 2024):
Subject: FMGC software P/12D — vertical navigation correction
Supersedes: EASA AD 2022-0201
Applies to: A320-200N and A321-271N with FMGC P/12C or earlier
Action: Install FMGC software standard P/12D within 6 months

EASA AD 2022-0201 (superseded):
Subject: FMGC software P/12C — previous vertical navigation update
Superseded by EASA AD 2024-0072

---

KEY TECHNICAL FEATURES

Fly-By-Wire: Full digital fly-by-wire flight control system (FBWC)
Flight Management: Honeywell or Thales FMGC (operator choice)
Avionics: Airbus LCD primary flight displays, integrated modular avionics (IMA)
Cabin: Airspace cabin standard (wider overhead bins, LED lighting)

---

PRIMARY OPERATORS (A320neo family)

IndiGo Airlines (India): Largest A320neo operator globally (500+ aircraft)
Air France: Primary European narrow-body replacement
Wizz Air: European LCC operator (all-A320neo family fleet)
Lufthansa Group: Mixed A320ceo and A320neo fleet
EasyJet: Transitioning from A320ceo to A320neo
Frontier Airlines, Spirit Airlines: North American LCC operators
Lion Air (Indonesia): Operates both A320neo and 737 MAX

---

MANUFACTURER INFORMATION

Airbus SAS is a subsidiary of Airbus SE (formerly EADS). Airbus manufactures aircraft
at facilities in Toulouse (France), Hamburg (Germany), Tianjin (China), and Mobile
(Alabama, USA). Airbus holds a 50% market share in the global narrow-body aircraft
segment, competing directly with the Boeing 737 family.
