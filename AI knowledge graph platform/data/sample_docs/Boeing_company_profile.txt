OVERVIEW

The Boeing Company is an American multinational aerospace and defense corporation.
Boeing Commercial Airplanes (BCA), headquartered in Renton, Washington, is responsible
for the design, manufacture, and support of commercial jet aircraft. Boeing is one of
the two largest manufacturers of commercial aircraft in the world, along with Airbus SAS.

---

CURRENT COMMERCIAL AIRCRAFT PROGRAMS

737 MAX Series (Next Generation successor)
- 737-7 (100-138 passengers): Entry into service 2021
- 737-8 (162-178 passengers): Entry into service 2017; grounded March 2019 — November 2020
- 737-9 (178-193 passengers): Entry into service 2021
- 737-10 (188-204 passengers): Certification pending as of 2024
Engine: CFM International LEAP-1B (sole engine option)
Key operators: Southwest Airlines, Ryanair, American Airlines, United Airlines, Air Canada

737 NG Series (predecessor to 737 MAX)
- 737-700, 737-800, 737-900ER
Engine: CFM International CFM56-7B
Status: Production ended; large fleet in service globally

787 Dreamliner Series
- 787-8, 787-9, 787-10
Engine: GE Aviation GEnx or Rolls-Royce Trent 1000 (operator choice)
Key features: 50% composite construction, reduced fuel burn vs. 767

777X Series
- 777-8, 777-9
Engine: GE Aviation GE9X (sole engine option)
Status: Certification ongoing as of 2024

REGULATORY RELATIONSHIPS

Primary Certification Authority: Federal Aviation Administration (FAA), United States
Bilateral Agreement Partners: EASA (European Union), TCCA (Canada), CAAC (China),
ANAC (Brazil), CAAS (Singapore) and 60+ other national authorities.

Boeing holds FAA Production Approval (PAH) and Design Approval Holder (DAH) status
for all Boeing commercial aircraft under 14 CFR Parts 21, 25, and 39.

---

737 MAX REGULATORY HISTORY

March 13, 2019: FAA Emergency Order of Airworthiness grounds all 737-8 and 737-9.
May 2020: FAA AD 2020-05-11 establishes return-to-service requirements.
November 18, 2020: FAA lifts grounding order; 737 MAX returns to US service.
March 2022: FAA AD 2022-03-07 requires additional AOA monitoring enhancements.
January 2024: FAA AD 2024-01-02 requires MCAS Software v2.0 installation.

MANUFACTURING

Primary assembly: Boeing Factory, Renton, WA (737 family)
Annual production rate (2024): Approximately 38 737 MAX aircraft per month
(target: 50/month by end 2025, subject to FAA oversight)
Major suppliers: Spirit AeroSystems (fuselage), Safran (landing gear), Parker Aerospace (hydraulics)
