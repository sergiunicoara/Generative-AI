AIRWORTHINESS DIRECTIVE
Federal Aviation Administration
14 CFR Part 39

Docket No.: FAA-2021-0855
AD No.: 2022-03-07

AGENCY: Federal Aviation Administration (FAA), DOT.
ACTION: Final rule.

SUMMARY: The FAA is adopting a new airworthiness directive (AD) for certain Boeing
Model 737-8 and 737-9 airplanes. This AD supersedes AD 2020-05-11. This AD was
prompted by additional mandatory requirements for continued airworthiness following
the return to service of the Boeing 737 MAX after the grounding period. This AD
requires enhanced MCAS software verification procedures, AOA disagree alert
activation, and mandatory crew training completion records.

DATES: This AD is effective March 25, 2022.

AFFECTED ADs: This AD supersedes AD 2020-05-11.

APPLICABILITY: This AD applies to Boeing Model 737-8 and 737-9 airplanes,
certificated in any category, with Line Numbers 1 through 4972, as identified in Boeing
Requirements Bulletin 737-31A1875 RB, dated October 12, 2021.

COMPLIANCE: Comply with this AD within the compliance times specified, unless already
done.

REQUIRED ACTIONS:
(a) MCAS Software Verification. Within 30 days after the effective date of this AD,
verify MCAS software version 1.7.3 or later is installed using AMM Task 31-21-00-
860-802. If software is not at required version, install updated software in accordance
with Boeing Service Bulletin 737-31-1887, Revision 1, dated February 2, 2022.

(b) AOA Disagree Alert. Before further flight, confirm activation of the AOA Disagree
alert system in accordance with Boeing Flight Deck Modification Document
737-31-1887-FDM, dated February 2, 2022. The AOA Disagree alert must annunciate
on the primary flight display when AOA sensor values diverge by more than 5.5 degrees.

(c) Flight Crew Training Records. Within 60 days after the effective date of this AD,
submit proof of completion of Boeing 737 MAX Differences Training for all line
qualified 737-8 and 737-9 pilots to the FAA Flight Standards District Office having
jurisdiction over your principal operations center.

(d) Repetitive Inspection. Every 2,000 flight cycles, perform AOA sensor bracket
inspection IAW AMM Task 27-41-00-200-803 and report results to FAA.
