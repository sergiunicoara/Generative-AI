BOEING 737 MAX
COMPONENT MAINTENANCE MANUAL (CMM)
CHAPTER 71 — POWER PLANT

Section: 71-21-05
Subject: CFM LEAP-1B Engine Mount — Inspection and Maintenance
Revision: 12
Date: November 15, 2023
Applicability: Boeing 737-8 and 737-9 aircraft equipped with CFM International LEAP-1B engines

---

1. GENERAL DESCRIPTION

The CFM LEAP-1B engine is mounted to the 737 MAX wing via a pylon assembly
comprising a forward mount fitting, aft mount fitting, and lateral brace link. The
LEAP-1B engine mount system differs from the CFM56-7B mount used on 737 NG aircraft
primarily in the forward mount geometry required to accommodate the larger fan diameter
(69.4 inches vs 61 inches on 737 NG).

The forward mount carries primary vertical and side loads. The aft mount carries
thrust and torque loads. The lateral brace link provides lateral load stability.

---

2. MAINTENANCE REQUIREMENTS

2.1 Visual Inspection — Forward Engine Mount
Interval: Every 600 flight cycles or 12 months, whichever occurs first.
Reference: AMM Task 71-21-05-200-801

Inspect the forward mount fitting for:
a. Cracks in the main load-path fitting (particularly at lug bore radii)
b. Corrosion on cadmium-plated surfaces
c. Loose or missing fasteners (torque check per AMM limits)
d. Bearing wear (measured with feeler gauge; replace if > 0.010 inch)

2.2 Detailed Inspection — Aft Mount Link Assembly
Interval: Every 3,000 flight cycles or on finding of any forward mount discrepancy.
Reference: AMM Task 71-21-05-200-802

Remove aft mount link assembly. Inspect link fitting bores for elongation using
bore gauge (maximum elongation: 0.005 inch over nominal). Inspect shear pins for
wear and corrosion. Replace if wear exceeds 0.003 inch.

2.3 Thrust Link Inspection
Interval: Every 1,500 flight cycles.
Reference: AMM Task 71-21-05-200-803

Inspect thrust link rod end bearings for radial play (maximum 0.012 inch).
Inspect rod end locking nut torque (refer to standard torque chart SSM 20-09-02).
Inspect link tube for impact damage or corrosion.

3. RELATED AIRWORTHINESS DIRECTIVES

The following ADs apply to LEAP-1B engine mount inspection:
- FAA AD 2024-01-02: References 737 MAX continued airworthiness requirements
- FAA AD 2022-03-07: AOA sensor bracket inspection includes forward pylon structure
- CFM AD CFM-2023-14: LEAP-1B fan blade inspection (separate document)

---

4. SPECIAL TOOLS REQUIRED

- Bore gauge: 0.001 inch resolution (P/N BG-737-01 or equivalent)
- Torque wrench: 0-150 ft-lb (calibrated within 12 months)
- Inspection mirror: 6-inch extended reach
- Dye penetrant kit: Turco Spray-X or equivalent (Pratt & Whitney spec C-204)

---

5. MANUFACTURER INFORMATION

CFM International (CFM) is a 50/50 joint venture between GE Aerospace (United States)
and Safran Aircraft Engines (France). The LEAP-1B engine entered commercial service
in 2017 with Malindo Air on the Boeing 737 MAX 8. As of 2024, CFM has delivered
over 2,400 LEAP-1B engines to 737 MAX operators worldwide.

Primary operators include Southwest Airlines (United States), Ryanair (Ireland),
Air Canada, Norwegian Air Shuttle, and Lion Air.
