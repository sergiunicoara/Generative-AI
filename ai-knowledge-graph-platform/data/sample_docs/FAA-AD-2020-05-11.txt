AIRWORTHINESS DIRECTIVE
Federal Aviation Administration
14 CFR Part 39

Docket No.: FAA-2020-0481
AD No.: 2020-05-11

AGENCY: Federal Aviation Administration (FAA), DOT.
ACTION: Final rule; request for comments.

SUMMARY: The FAA is adopting a new airworthiness directive (AD) for all Boeing
Model 737-8 and 737-9 airplanes. This AD was prompted by two fatal accidents involving
Boeing 737-8 airplanes — Lion Air Flight 610 on October 29, 2018, and Ethiopian
Airlines Flight 302 on March 10, 2019 — both of which involved erroneous activation
of the Maneuvering Characteristics Augmentation System (MCAS) due to faulty Angle
of Attack (AOA) sensor input. This AD requires installation of updated MCAS software
(version 1.7.2), activation of the AOA Disagree alert, enhanced pilot training, and
specific pre-flight checklist procedures.

DATES: This AD is effective May 27, 2020. Comments must be received by July 10, 2020.

APPLICABILITY: This AD applies to all Boeing Model 737-8 and 737-9 airplanes,
certificated in any category, with any line number, that are returned to service
following the FAA Emergency Order of Airworthiness dated March 13, 2019.

COMPLIANCE: Comply with this AD before return to revenue service of any affected
airplane.

REQUIRED ACTIONS:
(a) MCAS Software Update. Before return to service, install MCAS Software version
1.7.2 in accordance with Boeing Service Bulletin 737-31-1710, dated March 5, 2020.
The update modifies MCAS to: (1) use inputs from both AOA sensors rather than
alternating; (2) limit MCAS activation to one stabilizer nose-down command per
high-AOA event; (3) enable flight crew to override MCAS using electric stabilizer
trim switches.

(b) AOA Disagree Alert Activation. Before return to service, activate the AOA
Disagree alert system per Boeing Flight Deck Document 737-MAX-FDD-0001, Revision 3.
The alert must display on the primary flight display if left and right AOA sensor
readings disagree by more than 5.5 degrees.

(c) Pilot Training. Before operating any affected airplane, the certificate holder must
ensure all crewmembers complete Boeing 737 MAX Differences Training as outlined in
FAA Advisory Circular AC 120-53C. Training records must be retained for two years.

(d) Checklist Update. Before return to service, update the Quick Reference Handbook
(QRH) to include the Runaway Stabilizer checklist revision per Boeing Operations
Manual Bulletin TBC-19-11, Revision 3.

AUTHORITY: 49 USC 106(g), 40113, 44701; Emergency Order of Airworthiness
dated March 13, 2019.

the primary US operators of Boeing 737 MAX aircraft. Compliance is mandatory for
all Part 121 operators prior to revenue service resumption.
