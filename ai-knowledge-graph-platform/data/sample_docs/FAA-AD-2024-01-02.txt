AIRWORTHINESS DIRECTIVE
Federal Aviation Administration
14 CFR Part 39

Docket No.: FAA-2023-1842
AD No.: 2024-01-02

AGENCY: Federal Aviation Administration (FAA), DOT.
ACTION: Final rule.

SUMMARY: The FAA is adopting a new airworthiness directive (AD) for certain Boeing
Model 737-8 and 737-9 airplanes. This AD supersedes AD 2022-03-07. This AD was
prompted by updated safety analysis following the return to service of the Boeing 737
MAX fleet and a review of continued operational safety data. This AD requires updated
Maneuvering Characteristics Augmentation System (MCAS) software installation,
revised flight crew operating manual (FCOM) procedures, and enhanced angle-of-attack
(AOA) sensor monitoring.

DATES: This AD is effective February 15, 2024.

AFFECTED ADs: This AD supersedes AD 2022-03-07.

APPLICABILITY: This AD applies to Boeing Model 737-8 and 737-9 airplanes, certificated
in any category, as identified in Boeing Alert Requirements Bulletin (ARB) 737-31A1904
RB, dated December 5, 2023.

COMPLIANCE: Comply with this AD within the compliance times specified, unless already
done.

REQUIRED ACTIONS:
(a) Software Update. Within 90 days after the effective date of this AD, install MCAS
Software version 2.0 or later, in accordance with Boeing Service Bulletin 737-31-1944,
Revision 2, dated January 8, 2024.

(b) FCOM Revision. Before further flight after software installation, update the Flight
Crew Operations Manual to include revised MCAS procedures as specified in Boeing
Operations Manual Bulletin TBC-19-11, Revision 4.

(c) AOA Sensor Inspection. Within 500 flight hours after the effective date of this AD,
inspect the Angle of Attack sensor installation in accordance with Boeing AMM Task
27-41-00-200-801.

(d) Reporting. Within 30 days after accomplishing the actions required by paragraphs
(a) through (c) of this AD, report compliance to the FAA using the Compliance
Reporting Form available at https://av-info.faa.gov.
