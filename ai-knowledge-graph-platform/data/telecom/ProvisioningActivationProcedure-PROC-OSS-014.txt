PROVISIONING & ACTIVATION PROCEDURE
Document Reference: PROC-OSS-014
Version: 2.1
Effective Date: 2024-01-10
Scope: All circuit-dependent service provisioning and activation tasks

1. PURPOSE
Defines the standard workflow, validation requirements, and service-level
commitments for provisioning and activating services on operator-managed
network infrastructure (network elements, circuits, ports).

2. PROVISIONING WORKFLOW
  a. Service Order received and logged in OSS.
  b. Provisioning Task created; assigned Configuration Item(s) validated
     against the current CMDB Change Record (not against a cached
     Network Inventory Record snapshot — snapshots may be stale; see
     section 3).
  c. Activation Task executed once validation passes.
  d. Service Instance marked ACTIVE in CMDB.

3. CMDB VALIDATION REQUIREMENT
Before any Activation Task is executed, the assigned circuit or network
element's status MUST be re-validated against the latest CMDB Change
Record for that Configuration Item. A Network Inventory Record snapshot
is informational only and is NOT sufficient validation on its own —
it may not reflect changes recorded after its snapshot date.

4. DECOMMISSION VALIDATION CHECKLIST
Any circuit or network element decommission must:
  - Confirm no active Service Instance remains bound to the CI
  - Migrate or terminate dependent services before physical teardown
  - Close the associated CMDB Change Record with effective date and
    replacement CI reference (if applicable)

5. STANDARD ACTIVATION SLA
For all circuit-dependent provisioning and activation tasks, the
standard committed turnaround is 5 (five) business days from Service
Order receipt to Service Instance activation, measured from the date
the Provisioning Task passes CMDB validation (section 3). This SLA
applies uniformly across all subscriber tiers and MUST be reflected in
any Service Order's SLA commitment to the customer. Deviations require
written exception approval from the OSS Governance Board and must be
recorded on the Service Order itself.

6. AUTHORITY
This procedure is the binding operational standard for provisioning and
activation SLA commitments across all Service Orders. Where a Service
Order's stated SLA commitment conflicts with section 5 and no recorded
exception approval exists, this procedure's standard SLA governs.

Approved by: OSS Governance Board
